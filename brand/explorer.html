The Play

Crackpot to champion

Dance Star starts as one studio's scheduling front and grows into the place any dancer in America checks before Friday night. The brand has to survive all three stages without a rebrand, so it gets built for the last stage and worn casually in the first. These stages are a real sequence; the numbers mean something.

01

Crackpot

Phoenix only. Classes, socials, and instructors from Phoenix Bachata Dance and friends. The brand borrows trust through a quiet endorsement line and earns its first thousand dancers.

02

Challenger

Multi city. Studios list themselves, dancers follow scenes across state lines. Dance Star stands on its own name and its own color. The endorsement line retires.

03

Champion

The aggregator for the US dance community. Studios wear "a Dance Star studio" the way gyms wear ClassPass. The architecture flips and the parent becomes the platform.

Brand Architecture

Three models, one path

Dance Star can run as a parent brand or as a sub-brand of Phoenix Bachata Dance. The honest answer is both, staged over time. Each model below maps to a growth stage, and the visual rule that makes it work is the same in all three: Dance Star never wears Phoenix Bachata red (#E12330). Adjacency, never inheritance.

The flip

Stage 03 / Champion

Dance Star is the parent. Studios adopt the endorsement "a Dance Star studio" and keep their local colors, PBD red included. The platform palette stays untouchable so a thousand studio brands can sit inside it.
